Road Safety Awareness

Protecting Lives, Saving Futures

Why Road Safety Matters

  • Road accidents remain a significant public health challenge in Malaysia. On average, a road accident occurs every 50 seconds, and tragically, a life is lost every 1 hour and 56 minutes. These incidents cause emotional trauma, physical injuries, and substantial financial losses to families and the nation.

  • Despite improvements in emergency response and vehicle safety standards, the number of road accidents has seen a slight increase in recent years, highlighting the ongoing risks on Malaysian roads.

    • Key Road Safety Challenges

    • Poor driving habits such as distracted driving, speeding, and negligence.
    • High volume of vehicles, with over 36 million registered vehicles nationwide.
    • Motorcyclists, who make up 47% of vehicles, account for the majority of fatalities.
    • Inadequate road infrastructure for vulnerable road users.
    • Increased accidents during festive seasons due to traffic surges and fatigue.

    How We Can Improve Road Safety

    • Strengthening enforcement of traffic laws and penalties for violations.
    • Enhancing driver education to promote safe driving behaviors.
    • Improving road infrastructure with better lighting, signage, and speed-calming measures.
    • Utilizing smart traffic management and real-time data to identify accident hotspots.
    • Promoting public awareness campaigns, especially during high-risk periods like festivals.
